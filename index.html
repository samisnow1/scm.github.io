BREAKING FREE

Design Objective

The non-profit Breaking Free organization wanted to develop a website-based outreach program that could educate and empower female victims of sex trafficking, prostitution and/or sexual exploitation. In addition to providing these women with crucial educational content and valuable access to additional therapeutic resources, they also wanted to increase participation in a variety of afternoon and evening support groups being held by the organization in the Minneapolis/St. Paul area.


Device Focus

Breaking Free opted for a social media/PSA/video campaign in order to educate the community and reach out to vulnerable women in need of help. A video pop-up feature was then added to the home page.


Modern Design

The site’s modern design features big, bold type and photos, color contrasts of purple and green, and responsive design for all devices. The prototype for this website was designed first in Photoshop, and brought to life in InVision Studio.

MUELLER FAMILY MOVIE THEATER

Design Objective

Mueller Family Movie Theater’s website featured a very outdated design. So, while the initial goal was to create a modern-looking website, the owners also wanted a site designed to attract millennials and seniors.. In the UI/UX design phase, I created Photoshop mockups as well as a clickable prototype using OmniGraffle.


Device Focus

Market demographic research showed that of all possible devices the tablet was the best choice for reaching both target audiences (i.e., millennials and seniors). Also, the side-menu design featuring simple icons permitted easy site navigation by both user groups.


Flat Design

Because the owners of Mueller Family Movie Theater were big fans of modern web apps such as Netflix, I looked to the Netflix platform for design inspiration. Flat design, big buttons, dynamic colors, big type, and minimal design all came together in the final product.
